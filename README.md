Horizon Scanning is not about predicting the future. It is about detecting early warning signs of potential changes to better prepare for multiple possible futures.

"The future is not a destination, but a direction."

Module 4: The 6 Analytical Matrices (Included Templates)

This tool provides 6 templates. Here is how to use them effectively:

1. Impact vs. Probability (Risk Matrix)

Purpose: Traditional Risk Management.

Top-Right (High/High): Critical Risks. Mitigation is mandatory immediately.

Top-Left (High Imp/Low Prob): Black Swans. Requires insurance or emergency drills.

2. Impact vs. Uncertainty (Foresight Matrix)

Purpose: Scenario Planning (The most strategic view).

Top-Right (High/High): Scenario Candidates. These are the critical uncertainties that will define different futures.

Top-Left (High Imp/Low Unc): Planning Realities. These are "inevitable futures" (e.g., demographics). Plan for them now.

3. Urgency vs. Importance (Priority Matrix)

Purpose: Action Planning & Eisenhower Matrix.

Top-Right: Do First.

Bottom-Right: Schedule.

Top-Left: Delegate.

4. Influence vs. Interest (Stakeholder Mapping)

Purpose: Managing actors in an ecosystem.

Top-Right: Key Players. Manage closely.

Bottom-Right: Show Consideration. Keep informed.

5. Novelty vs. Maturity (Innovation Radar)

Purpose: Technology tracking.

Top-Left (High Novelty/Low Maturity): Bleeding Edge/R&D. High risk, high reward.

Bottom-Right: Commodity. Standard tech.

6. Time Horizon vs. Impact

Purpose: Roadmap alignment.

X-Axis: How soon will this hit? (Short/Medium/Long Term).

Y-Axis: How hard will it hit?

Module 5: Avoiding Cognitive Biases

When performing a scan, be aware of these mental traps:

Confirmation Bias: Only looking for signals that confirm what you already believe.

Solution: Actively hunt for contradicting evidence.

Availability Heuristic: Overestimating the importance of information that is readily available (e.g., breaking news).

Solution: Dig deeper into niche sources (academic papers, patent filings).

Groupthink: Agreeing with the consensus to avoid conflict.

Solution: Encourage "Wild Card" thinking in the matrix.
